Taught by someone who thinks in moves and probabilities.

Claude built the academy on a simple idea: chess is a language of information — every move narrows the possibilities, and the player who reads position best usually wins. That thinking shapes every lesson here, from a seven-year-old's first checkmate to a shopkeeper's lunchtime tactics puzzle.

Sessions run one-on-one or in small groups of up to four, in English, Kiswahili, or Luhya — whichever gets the idea across fastest. Bring your own board or use one of ours; either way, the clock at the door never stops.
